Robots Battle Arena

C | Python3 | Yacc & Lex

  • Text parser
  • Virtual Machine
  • Turns and Actions controller
  • Data piped into Pygame
  • Visual Interface

Semester long project, in which me and my group made a battle arena simulator from scratch. Players have a description of the map and several possible intructions. With five individual robots they must collect explosive cristals and place them on the enemy base, bofore that is done to their base.

Attack in groups, focus on defense, sneak in by the edges or go straight ahead. You make your strategy. You have to take care of your robots internal memory as that is a limited resource and be proactive as your turn end fast.

Code like you would do in Python. Your robot have memory, so you can set your variables. Memorizations also allows them to understand the functions you define. They see all things as targets or obstacles so they do not have classes. Yet.

Learning by Osmosis

C# | Unity

  • Local Multiplayer
  • Super Powers
  • Movement and bounds for chaotic gameplay
  • Voice acting

Game completely developed during The Global Game Jam. 2D Multiplayer Shooter game that puts the player in a extremely chaotic scenario. You have at least six lifes, but that will not last long as your enemies dash, go back in time and have multi-shot superpowers. Spend them wisely because at some point the new skills will matter much more than your survival.

You, as a student, has to throw your school books in your classmates. As you throw them, however, you also end up teaching them. Each subject will give a specific power. Too much knowledge, however, will knock them and take them out of the game.

Typhon

C# | Monogame Framework

  • Game made from scratch
  • First real game I made
  • Architecture Deffinition
  • Intense Code Review

Semester long project we did on Freshman year. I like to go back to it and see how much I learned and improved since then. I remember how me and my group felt brilliant when we solved all our references issues making a static class that held references to every object of the game.

Control Typhon, the ancient mytological spider. The "child" of a scientist is attacked by other creatures alike. Progress through the levels to battle stronger and faster enemies and special waves.

The Giving Kid

C# | Unity

  • Adventure storytelling
  • Stealth Mechanics

Game completely developed during the iThrive RIT Game Jam. The game Jam theme was kindness and how different it is from niceness. Spend a day in the life of a little kid who adventures and talk to strangers. Help him gladly give up his only lamp to help an artist with her creativity crisis. Help him give up his stuffed friend to an lonely old man!

Sneak out of the house steping as your parents snore! Drag the couch to reach the top shelf as you are still to little. All this while the game jam's winning sound plays in the background.
